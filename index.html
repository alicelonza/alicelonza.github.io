Tactical Resource Allocation for Healthcare Operations

Developed a tactical resource allocation model for the new St Jansdal outpatient clinic in Lelystad, focusing on consultation-room allocation across medical specialties under uncertain demand. The project combined queueing theory and discrete-event simulation to evaluate dedicated, fully pooled and hybrid room-sharing strategies. A total of 54 scenarios were analysed using performance indicators such as patient waiting times, room utilization, hospital closing time and overtime. Results demonstrated that hybrid pooling can significantly improve service levels while remaining operationally realistic.

Bachelor's Thesis

This thesis explored how operations research techniques can support the design of more efficient and sustainable public transport systems in low-density rural areas. Through the analysis of real-world case studies, I investigated how service frequency, network design, operational costs, and travel demand influence transport system performance.

The research focused on the application of optimization models to evaluate different public transport strategies, including conventional fixed-route services and demand-responsive transport solutions. Particular attention was given to balancing service accessibility, operational efficiency, and cost-effectiveness in areas where traditional public transport systems often struggle to remain viable.
